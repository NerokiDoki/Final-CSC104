Course Overview

This introductory course covers the essential concepts and techniques in the world of cybersecurity. Students will learn about common cyber threats, key security principles, and strategies for defending digital systems from attacks. It is designed for those looking to enter the field of cybersecurity or strengthen their understanding of how to protect personal and organizational information in the digital age.

Course Topics:

  • Introduction to Cybersecurity: Overview of the digital security landscape, including types of cyber threats and attacks.
  • Network Security: Understanding firewalls, VPNs, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and network protocols.
  • Data Protection: Best practices for safeguarding data, encryption methods, and securing storage systems.
  • Cyber Attack Methods: Common tactics used by cybercriminals including phishing, malware, ransomware, and social engineering.
  • Security Policies and Procedures: How to create, implement, and enforce security measures in organizations.
  • Incident Response and Recovery: How to detect, respond to, and recover from a security breach.

Course Format:

Duration: 6 weeks

Delivery Method: Online with weekly live sessions and interactive discussions.

Assessments: Quizzes, a final project on creating a security plan, and a hands-on lab where students simulate real-world cybersecurity attacks and defenses.

Prerequisites: Basic understanding of computers and networks.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Gain foundational knowledge of cybersecurity and common cyber threats.
  • Be able to recognize and mitigate basic security risks.
  • Understand the principles of protecting both personal and corporate data.
  • Be prepared to pursue more advanced studies in cybersecurity.
